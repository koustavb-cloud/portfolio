üèéÔ∏è Project: Combustion Go-Kart Build

Overview: A group project to design and assemble a functioning go-kart powered by a small combustion engine. The goal was to explore hands-on vehicle and chassis design, gain practical CAD, machining and system integration knowledge. This project emphasized problem-solving and learning through iterations rather than following a pre-defined plan (which would've been more useful)

What we did:

  • Design the chassis and basic model assembly on OnShape
  • Learned how to weld in order to be able to manufacture the chassis ourselves and save costs.
  • Installed a 255cc 4 stroke engine paired with a CVT to maximise torque output and fuel efficiency. Decision was made due to the fact that their was a huge weight balance on the car
  • Used bike brake cables to for throttle control and to implement a mechanical brake system
  • Showcased at Socratica Symposium in Waterloo, along with 70+ creators, with 1000+ in attendance

    • Problem we ran into:

  • A lot of overwelds, which meant grinding them down afterwards to give A) a clean look, and B) reduce any residual stresses caused by the overwelds
  • A recurring issue was the alignment for the engine sprocket and drive chain sprocket. Due to the absence of a positive-locking mechanism, the drive chain sprocket would misalign and shift while driving. In hindsight, a more permanent solution should have been implemented instead of relying on temporary fixes.
  • The vehicle struggled with weight distribution. Both engine and CVT were mounted behind the rear-axle, exhibiting instability due to a rear-heavy configuration. As a temporary measure, a thicker steel floor plate was installed to counterbalance the rear bias.
